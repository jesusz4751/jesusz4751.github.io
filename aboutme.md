# My Journey
From before my time at CSU Stanislaus, I always knew that I wanted to be in the forefront of technological advancements. While in my youth, I've always played with computers and learned about the newest technologies available. My family used to own a Windows 8 computer that could barely run Fortnite without crashing, and I would spend all my time learning the ins and outs of how the computer worked.

Early on in High School, I took online courses to learn how to code in Python. I was always interested in creating my own tools and shortcuts to optimize my life. I got into Tasker, an android automation tool, and learned about how I could automate everyday mundane tasks on my phone such as automatically playing music when I connect to my car or turning on vibrate mode when I leave the house. This led to me learning more about the concept of design thinking, which is a focus on designing programs and features that solve problems that real humans face. My coursework in two AP Computer Science classes and my first IT Support Internship as a high schooler further expanded on this interest, as both allowed me to learn about how to focus my technical skills on helping others solve their problems.

During my first year at Stanislaus State, I entered into a program known as ASPIRE. The program allowed me to do research as an undergraduate working for a professor at the university. I worked underneath Dr. Ayat Hatem, conducting research on misinformation and social network analysis on X users. This taught me more about humanities and how to use technology to solve real world problems, helping determine what causes misinformation to spread and how to stop it before it becomes viral. You can learn more about my experience on my <a href="research.html">research page.</a>

I've also worked on other personal projects outside of class, such as my food diet app. This app allows users to calculate what foods they can eat at 3 popular fast food chains based on how many calories they have left. This was the first web program I ever created and was a tool I created for my own life. I have a very active lifestyle, running and lifting multiple times a week, and a tool that would allow me to easily see what I can eat while still staying on top of my goals was something that would make my fitness journey much easier.

In 2025, I participated in StanHacks III to create a collaborative program alongside 3 others users. The goal of the program was to create a website that would benefit the community and help people connect with each other. We created a program called First Community, which allowed first responders and regular community members to connect with each other. First responders could post workshops and other activities for regular civilians to attend, and regular civilians could post their own events where they could invite first responders to attend and help make sure that the event was safe in the case of an emergency. Our program won the award for "Biggest Impact Improving Public Safety", and this led to me learning new technologies as well as how to collaboratively create a program with others on GitHub.

From here, I decided to pivot to Information Technology and Cybersecurity, as I found that helping others was the main thing that I would want to do in the future. I went through TryHackMe's Cyber Security 101 course, where I learned everything from the basics of networking and cryptography to common attacks used by hackers and how to protect against them. This taught me the fundamentals of computer technology and is what started me in my career in Information Technology.

I then went on to pursue my CompTIA Security+ Certification, teaching me about the fundamentals of cybersecurity and how I can ensure that any technical environment is designed to be secure from cybercriminals. I am now studying for my CompTIA Network+ Certification through Calbright Community College, which teaches me more about the technicalities behind networking and how to do everything from installing new devices to setting up routers and networks to function properly. I am working to expand my technical skills to become a valuable asset in the Information Technology field.
